### [1] [Generalized Extended Codes with Applications in Entanglement-Assisted Qubit and Qutrit Codes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02170)
*Yang Li,Martianus Frederic Ezerman,Shitao Li,San Ling,Zhonghua Sun*

Main category: cs.IT

TL;DR: This paper proves that generalized extended codes are monomially equivalent to Hermitian duals of certain codes, characterizes Hermitian hull and dual distance of such codes, and uses these results to construct 267 new EA qubit codes and 14 new EA qutrit codes.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To systematically construct entanglement-assisted quantum error-correcting codes (EAQECCs) with improved parameters by controlling Hermitian hull dimension and dual distance through generalized extended codes.

Method: Establishes monomial equivalence between generalized extended codes and Hermitian duals of extended codes; characterizes Hermitian hull and dual distance via position of vector u relative to C + C^{⊥_H} and its interaction with minimum weight codewords; applies to Hermitian construction for EAQECCs.

Result: Obtained 267 new EA qubit codes (n ≤ 40) and 14 new EA qutrit codes (n ≤ 25); among them, 236 qubit and 8 qutrit codes show confirmed improvements over best-known codes.

Conclusion: The proposed framework enables independent control of Hermitian hull dimension and dual distance, yielding a significant number of improved EAQECCs beyond existing literature.

Abstract: We prove that any generalized extended code is monomially equivalent to the Hermitian dual of a code which is closely related to a second kind of extended code of $\C^{\perp_{\rm H}}$. Every $[n+1,k+1]_{q^2}$ linear code $\D$ with $d(\D^{\perp_{\rm H}})>1$ is monomially equivalent to the generalized extended code $\C({\bf u},a)$ of an $[n,k]_{q^2}$ linear code $\C$ for a fixed $a\in\F_{q^2}^{*}$ and some ${\bf u}\in\F_{q^2}^{n}$. We then characterize the Hermitian hull and Hermitian dual distance of $\C({\bf u},a)$ in terms of the position of ${\bf u}$ relative to $\C+\C^{\perp_{\rm H}}$ and the interaction between ${\bf u}$ and the minimum weight codewords of $\C^{\perp_{\rm H}}$, respectively. We obtain explicit criteria to independently control the expected Hermitian hull dimension and Hermitian dual distance of $\C({\bf u},a)$. In particular, several conditions for simultaneously increasing the Hermitian hull dimension and the Hermitian dual distance of $\C({\bf u},a)$ are derived. Applying these results to the Hermitian construction for EAQECCs gives us $267$ new EA qubit codes of lengths $n \leq 40$ and $14$ new EA qutrit codes of lengths $n \leq 25$ compared to the best-known codes in Grassl's code tables and the imporvements recorded in very recent works in the literature. Among the new parameter sets, we confirm improvements for $236$ qubit and $8$ qutrit codes.

### [2] [The Weight Distribution of the Third-Order Reed-Muller Code of Length 2048](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02365)
*Kirill Khoruzhii,Patrick Gelß,Sebastian Pokutta*

Main category: cs.IT

TL;DR: This paper computes the weight distribution of RM(3,11) by analyzing coset weight enumerators of 3.69 million GL(10,2)-orbits of cubic forms, using a structural theorem about nondegenerate cubic forms. It also determines the second-order nonlinearity of all cubic forms (covering radius 408), and improves bounds on covering radii for RM(2,10) and RM(6,10).


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To compute the weight distribution of RM(3,11) and determine the second-order nonlinearity of Boolean cubic forms, which are fundamental problems in coding theory with applications to cryptography and combinatorial designs.

Method: The computation enumerates coset weight enumerators of f+RM(2,10) for representatives of all 3,691,560 nonzero GL(10,2)-orbits of Boolean cubic forms. A structural theorem about nondegenerate cubic forms admitting a nondegenerate hyperplane restriction (except for one orbit per odd dimension) enables the computation.

Result: - The relative covering radius of RM(2,10) in RM(3,10) is 408, attained on 179 orbits (improving from 400 to 408). - The relative covering radius of RM(6,10) in RM(7,10) is at most 32 (improving from 50).

Conclusion: The weight distribution of RM(3,11) is fully determined, and improved bounds on covering radii of Reed-Muller codes are established through both exhaustive algebraic computation and complementary heuristic search.

Abstract: We compute the weight distribution of the third-order Reed--Muller code RM(3,11) of length 2048. The weight enumerator is assembled from the coset weight enumerators of f+RM(2,10), evaluated for representatives of all 3691560 nonzero GL(10,2)-orbits of Boolean cubic forms in ten variables. The computation rests on a structural theorem: a nondegenerate Boolean cubic form admits a nondegenerate hyperplane restriction, except for a single orbit in each odd dimension. The same pass determines the second-order nonlinearity of every cubic form: the relative covering radius of RM(2,10) in RM(3,10) is 408, attained on 179 orbits. This raises the best known lower bound on the covering radius of RM(2,10) from 400 to 408. A complementary heuristic search shows that the relative covering radius of RM(6,10) in RM(7,10) is at most 32, improving the previous bound of 50.

### [3] [Generalized Rank Weight and Extended Generalized Poset Weight Defined For Codes Over Rings: A Galois Connection Approach](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02377)
*Yang Xu,Haibin Kan,Guangyue Han*

Main category: cs.IT

TL;DR: This paper studies generalized rank weights and extended generalized poset weights of codes over rings using Galois connections, unifying and generalizing various known results including Singleton bounds, Wei-type duality theorems, and characterizations of MDS/MRD codes.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To unify and generalize existing results on generalized weights, rank weights, and poset weights for codes over rings (beyond just fields) using the abstract framework of Galois connections.

Method: Uses Galois connection theory to reformulate coding-theoretic properties; generalizes GRWs and rank profiles to modules over principal ideal rings (especially chain rings); introduces EGPWs and extended poset profiles for modules with composition series over quasi-Frobenius rings.

Result: Reformulates properties like Singleton bound, MDS/MRD characterizations, evasive property, and security drops via Galois connections; establishes Singleton bound and Wei-type duality for GRWs over chain rings; generalizes scattered bound for evasive codes; unifies two Wei-type duality theorems for EGPWs over quasi-Frobenius rings.

Conclusion: The Galois connection approach provides a unified framework for studying various generalized weight concepts over rings, extending results from fields to more general algebraic structures.

Abstract: In this paper, we study generalized rank weights (GRWs) and extended generalized poset weight (EGPWs) of codes over rings via a Galois connection approach. First, we show that various coding-theoretic properties related to generalized weights, including security drops of a code employed in wire-tap channel of type II, connections between generalized weights of a Gabidulin code and its associated Delsarte code, (generalized) Singleton bound, MDS discrepancy of a code, characterizations of MDS, near MDS, $i$-MDS, MRD, near MRD, $i$-MRD, (dually) quasi-MRD codes as well as evasive property of subspaces, can be reformulated in terms of Galois connections. Next, we study GRWs and rank profiles defined for modules over principal ideal rings, especially those over chain rings. Generalizing GRWs defined for vector spaces over fields, we establish a singleton bound and a Wei-type duality theorem, characterize MRD, near MRD and dually quasi-MRD codes and determine their GRWs; moreover, we characterize $i$-MRD codes and establish a scattered bound for $(h,h)$-evasive codes over chain rings, generalizing counterpart result established for vector space over finite fields. Finally, we propose and study EGPWs and extended poset profiles defined for modules with a composition series, which in fact form a Galois connection. Generalizing EGPWs defined for modules over finite Galois rings, we establish a Wei-type duality theorem for modules over arbitrary quasi-Frobenius rings, which unifies the two Wei-type duality theorems derived in both \cite{32} and \cite{33}.

</details>

### [4] [Integrated Wake-Up Radio and MIMO Solution for Cellular IoT Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02008)
*Israa Khaled,Ammar El Falou,Nour Kouzayha,Charlotte Langlais*

Main category: eess.SP

TL;DR: This paper investigates MIMO beamforming for wake-up radio in cellular IoT networks, showing it significantly improves wake-up reliability, reduces false activations by over 50%, and extends device battery life compared to single-antenna baselines.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To enhance energy efficiency and extend battery life of IoT devices by using MIMO beamforming with wake-up radio, moving beyond the typical single-antenna base station assumptions in existing WUR research.

Method: Develops a stochastic geometry-based analytical framework to evaluate wake-up success probability for WUR-MIMO in multi-cell cellular IoT networks, using an antenna configuration of 2×(number of devices)−1, validated via Monte Carlo simulations.

Result: MIMO beamforming significantly improves wake-up reliability, mitigates more than 50% of false activations across all settings, and prolongs IoT device lifetime compared to single-antenna WUR baseline.

Conclusion: MIMO beamforming is effective for enhancing WUR performance in IoT networks, offering substantial gains in energy efficiency and coverage extension.

Abstract: Wake-up radio (WUR) is a technology designed to enhance the energy efficiency of Internet of Things (IoT) networks and extend device battery life. While most studies focus on WUR performance with single-antenna base stations, this paper investigates the multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) technology to improve device energy saving and extend the coverage of wake-up signals. By leveraging MIMO beamforming, the transmitted energy can be spatially focused toward the intended IoT devices, with high beamforming gain and minimal inter-device interference. We develop a preliminary analytical framework using stochastic geometry to evaluate the wake-up success probability of WUR-MIMO in multi-cell cellular IoT networks, when the number of antennas equals $2 \times (\text{number of devices}) - 1$. Monte Carlo simulations show that, relative to a single-antenna WUR baseline, MIMO beamforming significantly enhances wake-up reliability when this antenna configuration is applied, mitigates more than 50% of false activations across all settings, and thereby prolongs the lifetime of IoT devices.

</details>


### [5] [Antenna System for Simultaneous Wireless Power and Information Transfer to Brain Implants](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02036)
*Ali Khaleghi,Aminolah Hassanvand,Ilangko Balasingham*

Main category: eess.SP

TL;DR: A battery-free wireless BCI system using dual-function antenna for inductive power transfer and backscatter communication (up to 32 Mbps), eliminating wires and batteries for safer neural interfaces.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Traditional implantable BCIs rely on wired connections and invasive microelectrode arrays with batteries, posing infection risks, mobility constraints, and safety concerns. A wireless, battery-free solution is needed.

Method: The system uses a dual-function antenna: (1) inductive coupling for wireless power transfer to run the ASIC for stimulation/readout, and (2) a backscatter antenna for high-data-rate (32 Mbps) battery-free wireless communication.

Result: Achieves battery-free operation with wireless power transfer and high-data-rate backscatter communication up to 32 Mbps, maintaining data fidelity and energy efficiency.

Conclusion: The proposed system eliminates wires and batteries, providing a safer, more efficient BCI interface suitable for applications like robotic arm control.

Abstract: Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs) have revolutionized neuroscience applications, from motor rehabilitation to neuroergonomics. Traditional implantable BCIs with invasive microelectrode arrays pose challenges, notably the need for wired connections and inherent implantation risks. This paper introduces a battery-free wireless BCI system, consolidating an implant and its external supporting system. Our design centers on a dual-function antenna system: firstly, an inductive coupling mechanism enables wireless power transfer, sufficiently powering the implant's Application-Specific Integrated Circuit (ASIC) for stimulation and readout without an implant battery. Secondly, a backscatter antenna in the implant facilitates battery-free, high-data-rate wireless connectivity (up to 32 Mbps). This system not only enhances the BCI experience by eliminating wires but also retains data fidelity and energy efficiency, promising a safer, more efficient interface for tasks like robotic arm control.

</details>
